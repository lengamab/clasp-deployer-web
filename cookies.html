Cookie Policy

Last Updated: December 25, 2025

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how ScriptFlow ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you use our application. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

Quick Summary: We use cookies and local storage to provide essential functionality, remember your preferences, and improve your experience with ScriptFlow.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website or use an application. They are widely used to make applications work more efficiently and provide useful information to application developers.

2.1 Types of Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-Party Cookies: Cookies set by ScriptFlow
  • Third-Party Cookies: Cookies set by external services we integrate with (e.g., Google)

3. How We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

ScriptFlow uses cookies and similar technologies (including browser local storage and session storage) for the following purposes:

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the application to function properly. Without them, certain features would not work.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
session_token Maintains your session when using the application Session
auth_state Stores authentication state for Google OAuth Session
csrf_token Security token to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks Session

3.2 Preference Cookies

These cookies remember your choices and preferences to provide a personalized experience.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
theme_preference Remembers your theme choice (light/dark/system) 1 year
ide_preference Stores your preferred IDE settings 1 year
language_preference Remembers your language selection 1 year

3.3 Analytics Cookies (If Enabled)

These cookies help us understand how you use the application so we can improve it. Analytics features are optional and can be disabled.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
_analytics_id Anonymous identifier for usage analytics 2 years
_session_tracking Tracks session duration and feature usage Session

4. Local Storage and Session Storage

In addition to cookies, ScriptFlow uses browser local storage and session storage to store:

  • API Keys: Your automation platform API keys (Make.com, Zapier, n8n)
  • OAuth Tokens: Google authentication tokens for Apps Script
  • Project Data: Your project configurations and metadata
  • Application State: Current application state and user interface preferences
  • Settings: User preferences and configuration settings
Important: Local storage data remains on your device and is not transmitted to our servers. This ensures your sensitive credentials stay secure on your local machine.

5. Third-Party Cookies

When you use ScriptFlow, you may encounter cookies from third-party services we integrate with:

5.1 Google Services

When authenticating with Google OAuth for Apps Script integration, Google may set cookies. These cookies are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

  • Cookie Policy: Google Cookie Policy
  • Privacy Policy: Google Privacy Policy

5.2 Automation Platforms

When connecting to Make.com, Zapier, or n8n, these services may use their own cookies. Please review their respective cookie policies:

  • Make.com Cookie Policy
  • Zapier Privacy Policy
  • n8n Privacy Policy

6. Managing Cookies

6.1 Browser Settings

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

6.2 Browser-Specific Instructions

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies

6.3 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that if you disable or refuse cookies, some features of ScriptFlow may not function properly:

  • Authentication: You may not be able to stay logged in to Google services
  • Preferences: Your settings and preferences will not be saved
  • Session Management: You may need to re-authenticate frequently
  • Functionality: Certain features may become unavailable

7. Managing Local Storage

To clear local storage data in ScriptFlow:

  1. Open your browser's Developer Tools (usually F12)
  2. Navigate to the "Application" or "Storage" tab
  3. Select "Local Storage" or "Session Storage"
  4. Find localhost:3002
  5. Right-click and select "Clear" or delete individual items

Warning: Clearing local storage will delete your stored API keys and project configurations. You will need to re-enter this information.

8. Do Not Track

Some browsers support a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. ScriptFlow does not currently respond to DNT browser signals, but we provide you with the ability to opt out of analytics tracking through application settings.
